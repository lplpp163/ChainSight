Three helpers, one dashboard

Three AI helpers that turn a factory floor into a live signal — in plain English.

No acronyms. No rip-and-replace. A $240 box plugs into whatever PLCs and cameras the supplier already owns, and within 72 hours the big client can see them in real time.

01 · Watches

Pulse.

Live machine status, live yield, live delivery ETA — refreshed every second.

A small computer at each supplier watches the machines. It classifies every second as running, idle, faulted, or in maintenance. The big client sees one tile per supplier: "Line 3 at 87%, order #4471 on time for Monday 14:00."

CH. 05 Technical Architecture
$240 edge box · raw data stays on site · rides CHT's own cables back to Taiwan.
07 / 14
Built for the tropics, built for the law

A $240 box. Five steps. Your data never leaves the factory.

Designed around Southeast Asia's three hardest constraints — tropical heat, blocked 5G spectrum, Malaysia's new privacy law — not a generic cloud template.

01.
A small rugged computer
An $240 industrial box (NVIDIA Jetson) with Wi-Fi 6, mobile backup, and an IP65 case that handles 40°C + humidity. Plugs into the sensors the supplier already owns.
02.
AI runs on the factory floor
Pulse and Predict run inside the factory, not in the cloud. Raw yield data, machine images, operator logs — none of it ever crosses the factory wall.
03.
Only summary numbers travel
The box sends a small encrypted packet (less than 2 KB) with status codes and KPIs. Everything else stays on the supplier's premises.
04.
CHT's own pipes carry it
The packet travels over CHT's private network, then a CHT-owned undersea cable to Taiwan — not AWS, not Azure, not a Chinese link.
05.
Fab sees a live tile
The big client opens a dashboard in CHT's own Taiwan data centre. Backed by the same security team that protects Taiwan's government systems.
The SE Asia constraints we designed around

Three things no generic cloud can solve.

1 · No private 5G. Malaysia's regulator gave 5G rights to a single local body, and foreign operators can't get spectrum. So we built the base layer on Wi-Fi 6 — it works, it's cheap, it's legal everywhere in the region.

2 · Data privacy fines hit in June 2025. Malaysia's new law means a €1M-equivalent penalty for any raw production data that leaves the factory. Our answer: nothing leaves except summary KPIs.

3 · Tropical conditions. The hardware is sealed for 40°C + 85% humidity, has backup power, and keeps working for up to 72 hours if the connection drops.

Material risks and mitigations

Five material risks, each with a planned mitigation.

The five risks below span regulation, customer value, technology, and integration. Each has a named owner and a concrete mitigation already scheduled in the 18-month plan.

5G spectrum
Malaysia's regulator gave 5G rights to a single local body. Foreign operators cannot get spectrum. A "5G smart factory" story would be dead on arrival.
Wi-Fi 6 is the base layer. 5G is an optional upgrade via a local partnership with Celcom. Nothing in the product depends on CHT owning Malaysian spectrum.
Data privacy
Malaysia's new privacy act (June 2025) makes raw production data leaving the factory a seven-figure fine. Chip-maker NDAs are even stricter.
Raw data never leaves the plant. Only anonymised summary numbers travel — and only over CHT's own cables. We appointed a data-protection officer in the Malaysia subsidiary from day one.
Why pay
Will a big chip factory actually pay $5–15K a month? The procurement team needs a number that clears the hurdle rate.
3.2× return. Four missed orders a year × $250K each = $1M of loss avoided. Subscription is $120K a year. The math clears procurement on the first pass.
AI cold-start
A brand-new supplier has no historical data. How does the AI work on day one?
We pre-trained on three years of ASE Taiwan data — 18 million labelled events. A new supplier warms up in 72 hours on that model and fine-tunes on local data after 30 days.
ERP integration
Connecting to SAP or Oracle is slow, expensive, and the talent is scarce. A big chunk of the budget could disappear there.
Phase 1–3 does not touch ERP. We read directly from the factory-floor controllers. ERP connectors are a Phase 5 upsell through system-integrator partners.
The full risk register is maintained jointly by CHT Malaysia's DPO and the ChainSight operations lead, and is reviewed each quarter
