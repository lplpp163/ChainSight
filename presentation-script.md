## [3] THE PROBLEM — THE VISIBILITY GAP — ~2 min

These massive fabs — Intel, ASE, Infineon — depend on **thousands of small local suppliers**. There are over **3,000 Tier 2 and Tier 3 SMEs** in the Penang semiconductor ecosystem making connectors, doing sub-assembly, supplying raw materials.

Here's the problem: these SMEs still run on **Excel spreadsheets and phone calls**. Only **18% have adopted any kind of Industry 4.0 technology**.  
*(These figures are from our team's research. ⚠️ The 18% figure should be verified with a primary source for the final deck.)*

So when Intel asks a Penang supplier "what's your real capacity right now?" — it takes **48 hours** to get an answer. By then the opportunity may have already gone elsewhere. There's a massive visibility gap between the big fabs and their small suppliers.

This matters even more now because the industry is shifting from **Just-in-Time to Just-in-Case**. According to SEMI's 2026 member survey, Taiwan's semiconductor industry is moving from pursuing extreme efficiency toward resilience and safety buffers. **80% of OSAT companies** listed Malaysia as a priority relocation destination.  
*(Source: SEMI annual member survey, reported by Bonny's research)*

Companies don't just want efficiency — they want **resilience**. They want to see problems coming before they happen. But you can't predict what you can't see.

Malaysia also faces a **talent gap** — the country needs approximately 60,000 engineers but produces only around 5,000 per year.  
*(⚠️ This is on our idea brief but the original source is unverified. Use carefully or remove.)*

AI can fill this gap — transforming raw IoT signals into actionable decisions that would otherwise require engineers who simply aren't available.

## [4] THE SOLUTION — CHAINSIGHT — ~3 min

ChainSight closes this visibility gap. It's a lightweight AI platform designed specifically for semiconductor SME suppliers — companies with 50 to 500 employees that can't afford big enterprise systems like SAP.

**Important positioning**: ChainSight is **not another ERP**. It fills the real-time gap between ERP (which is always lagging) and the actual factory floor (which is live) via 5G IoT. We **complement** existing systems like SAP. We don't compete with them.

We have three AI modules:

### Module 1: Pulse — Real-Time Monitoring
- 5G IoT sensors on the factory floor connected through CHT's private network
- Real-time OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness), yield data, machine status — all visible on a client portal
- AI auto-classifies machine status
- The fab can see live production data from their suppliers — no more phone calls, no more waiting 48 hours

### Module 2: Predict — AI Forecasting
- Machine learning models analyze sensor data and **predict problems before they happen**
- Equipment failure predicted **7 to 14 days** in advance
- Material depletion warnings before stockouts
- True delivery date estimates based on real production data, not optimistic promises
- The fab sees this on their dashboard and can react before it becomes a crisis

### Module 3: Comply — Automated Compliance
- Auto-maps factory data to **RBA** (Responsible Business Alliance), **CSRD** (EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive), and **Malaysia OSH Act** standards
- **One-click audit reports** — what currently takes 3 people × 2 months of paperwork
- The SME saves time and money; the fab gets guaranteed compliance from every supplier

## [5] BUSINESS MODEL & INNOVATION — ~2 min

Our business model is what makes this work. We call it **"Fabs pay, SMEs deploy free."**

### Side A — Fabs (the payers):
- Intel, ASE, Infineon, Inari pay **$5,000–15,000/month** for the visibility portal
- They get a dashboard showing real-time status of all their suppliers, predictive alerts, and compliance verification

### Side B — SMEs (zero upfront):
- 3,000+ Penang SMEs get onboarded at **zero upfront cost**
- Monthly fee of **$800–2,000** for AI + compliance features
- Why so affordable? Because the fab is essentially subsidizing the deployment. Intel *wants* their suppliers on this platform — it saves Intel millions in supply chain disruptions

### The Flywheel (Network Effect):
More suppliers on the platform → more visibility for the fab → fabs willing to pay more → more revenue to onboard more suppliers. **Competitors without telecom infrastructure cannot replicate this flywheel.**

### Cross-Border Value:
A Taiwanese factory owner sitting in Taipei can see their Penang supplier's production in real-time — data flowing through **CHT's submarine cables** and secured by **CHT's cybersecurity subsidiary**. We call this the **digital umbilical cord** between Taiwan headquarters and Malaysian factories.

**For Taiwanese firms specifically**: ChainSight provides secure cross-border factory control under Taiwan+1 relocation — addressing a need that no local Malaysian vendor or Western cloud provider can match with the same trust level.

## [6] WHY ONLY CHT — ~2 min

This is the critical question. Why can't someone else do this?

CHT has **five capabilities that no single competitor combines**:

1. **Connectivity Expertise (WiFi 6E → 5G roadmap)** — Phase 1 deploys on **WiFi 6E/7 with edge computing** — fast, no spectrum licensing, immediately deployable. As Malaysia's private 5G framework matures (MCMC has opened the **26.5–28.1 GHz mmWave band** on a first-come-first-served basis for private networks; the Digital Ministry is actively pushing a new private 5G rollout policy for manufacturing), we upgrade to 5G for advanced use cases. CHT's 5G-Advanced expertise from Taiwan (MoUs with Nokia and Ericsson at MWC 2026) positions us to lead when private 5G opens fully.  
   *(Sources: [MCMC](https://www.mcmc.gov.my/en/media/press-releases/pelaksanaan-rangkaian-5g-kedua-malaysia); [Bernama — Digital Ministry private 5G policy](https://bernama.com/en/news.php?id=2299762); [Light Reading — Malaysia spectrum allocation](https://www.lightreading.com/5g/malaysia-outlines-innovative-approach-to-5g-spectrum-allocation); note: Clarion Malaysia in Penang already deployed Malaysia's first 5G-enabled manufacturing line via YTL's Yes 5G Private Network, proving the model works — [Digital News Asia](https://www.digitalnewsasia.com/business/clarion-malaysia-debuts-malaysias-first-ai-and-robotics-based-advanced-manufacturing))*

2. **iEN IoT Platform** — CHT's proven smart energy and IoT platform already in production in Taiwan.

3. **14+ Submarine Cables** — CHT owns submarine cables connecting Taiwan to Southeast Asia, including the **AUG East cable** landing in Taiwan, Singapore, Indonesia, **Malaysia**, Brunei, Philippines, Korea, and Japan (~8,900 km, built by NEC).  
   *(Source: CHT submarine cable announcement 2026/03/21)*

4. **Cybersecurity Subsidiary + Data Compliance Architecture** — End-to-end encryption. Critically, our architecture is designed for Malaysia's **PDPA (amended 2024-2025)**: personal data (e.g., worker camera footage) stays on **edge compute in Malaysia** and is anonymized before any cross-border transfer. Only aggregated operational data (machine telemetry, OEE, yield) — which is not personal data under PDPA — flows to CHT's AIDC in Taiwan via submarine cable. Cross-border transfers of any personal data require a **Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA)** under the new PDPA guidelines (effective April 2025), and Taiwan's own PDPA provides a basis for the "substantially similar" law standard Malaysia requires.  
   *(Sources: [Hogan Lovells — MY cross-border data transfer guidelines](https://www.hoganlovells.com/en/publications/malaysias-groundbreaking-cross-border-data-transfer-guidelines-explained); [CMS LawNow](https://cms-lawnow.com/en/ealerts/2025/06/malaysian-guidelines-on-cross-border-data-transfers); [Mayer Brown](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2025/07/from-legislative-reform-to-practical-guidance-key-amendments-to-malaysias-pdpa-and-the-launch-of-cross-border-transfer-guidelines))*
   
   **Bonus opportunity**: Malaysia is building a **RM2 billion sovereign AI cloud** under MCMC (announced in 2026 budget). ChainSight's Malaysia-side processing could deploy on this sovereign cloud — aligning with the government's data sovereignty goals while strengthening our relationship with Malaysian regulators.  
   *(Source: [Lowyat.NET](https://www.lowyat.net/2025/369197/budget-2026-mcmc-sovereign-ai-cloud-rm2-billion-investment/); [Sinar Daily](https://www.sinardaily.my/article/730888/focus/national/mcmc-to-build-rm2-billion-sovereign-ai-cloud-under-malaysias-ai-nation-2030-vision))*

5. **CHT Malaysia Subsidiary (est. Dec 2025)** — Local presence in Penang, KL, and Johor. Already on the ground with sales and support capability.

**Proven track record**: CHT already built 5G smart factories for **CHPT** (CHT's own subsidiary — 1,000+ AIoT sensors, real-time chemical analysis), **ASE** (Taiwan's first 5G mmWave smart factory, with Qualcomm), and **Delta Electronics**.  
*(Source: DIGITIMES reporting)*

The bottom line: **CHT does the infrastructure layer that SAP cannot. The domain AI is co-built with semiconductor partners.** Proven tech, no invention needed.

ASUS can sell cameras. Claireye can sell AI software. Siemens can sell automation. Local telcos can sell connectivity. **Only CHT can deliver all of it as one integrated service**, from a company that Taiwanese manufacturers already trust.

## QUESTIONS FOR MENTORS / JUDGES

### Q1: Go-to-Market Entry
Our plan has fabs (Intel, ASE, Infineon) as the paying customers. In your experience with CHT's enterprise sales, **what's the most realistic path to getting the first fab signed on** — through CHT Malaysia's existing contacts, through Taiwan government trade channels, or through a direct approach to the fab's supply chain management team?

### Q2: SME Adoption Risk
The biggest risk is that SMEs resist deploying IoT sensors on their factory floors, even at zero cost — either from data privacy concerns or simple inertia. **What incentive structures or trust-building approaches have worked in CHT's past IoT deployments** (e.g., CHPT, ASE, Delta) to overcome this resistance?

### Q3: Pricing Validation
We priced the fab portal at $5K–15K/month and SME services at $800–2K/month. **Does this pricing feel right for the Malaysian market**, or should we adjust? Is there a risk of underpricing the fab side or overpricing the SME side?

### Q4: Compliance as Revenue Driver
The Comply module (auto-generating RBA/CSRD/OSH audit reports) is our Phase 3 feature. Some of our research suggests compliance could be the feature that **fabs care about most** because it reduces their own audit costs. **Should we move Comply earlier in the roadmap**, or is the Pulse → Predict → Comply sequence correct?

### Q5: Connectivity in Malaysia
Our Phase 1 deploys on WiFi 6E with edge computing to avoid 5G spectrum licensing complexity. We know Malaysia's 5G landscape is in transition — DNB, the dual-network model, U Mobile as the second network. **What's the realistic timeline for deploying 5G private networks in Malaysian industrial parks?** Should we plan to partner with a local operator like YTL (who already deployed 5G for Clarion in Penang) rather than waiting for CHT to obtain spectrum directly?

### Q6: Data Compliance
Malaysia's PDPA was significantly amended in 2024-2025 with new cross-border data transfer rules requiring Transfer Impact Assessments. Malaysia is also building a RM2 billion sovereign AI cloud. Our architecture keeps personal data on edge compute in Malaysia and only sends anonymized operational data to Taiwan. **Is this architecture sufficient, or should we plan to host ALL processing within Malaysia** (e.g., on the sovereign AI cloud) to be safe?

### Q7: Competitive Response
If ChainSight gains traction, established players like Siemens or local Malaysian IT firms could enter. **What's the strongest defensibility argument** — is it the cross-border submarine cable connectivity, the fab-subsidized flywheel, or something else we should emphasize?
